### Interpretive Paraphrase (Maximum Plausibility)

Based on comparison with Galenic herbal monographs on Vitis (grape) from Dioscorides and medieval herbals:

> **Recipe for the Vine [Grape].**
>
> Take the herb of this preparation and the plant compound. This compound of this kind is given in the third degree.
>
> The herb with this property of the plant: **it is hot in the first degree**. One ounce of the plant. The attribute is one scruple of the plant, and mix the compound.
>
> In the first degree, of which the plant preparation [is made]. [The leaf/tendril] is [beneficial]. This botanical in botanical, botanical, with the plant compound of this.
>
> The botanical compound with the attribute: this, this, this herb with -- **it is hot**. The botanical water, the plant property in the third degree, the botanical attribute **in the second degree**.
>
> A quantity of the plant, the preparation, this, and the plant-compound preparation. The botanical property, the root water, with: **is the cold property** of this compound, **in the first and second degree**.
>
> The botanical compound, the [specified] property, and [thus concludes].

### Commentary

This flowing translation suggests a **Galenic monograph** describing:
1. **Plant identification**: "Take the herb" (pchair)
2. **Humoral qualities**: "It is hot" (ckhey), degrees 1-3 mentioned
3. **Temperature properties**: Both hot (ckhey) and cold (otcho) mentioned -- consistent with grape having both warm and cool applications (wine = warming, grape juice = cooling)
4. **Dosage**: Scruple (qokain), ounce (qokaiin) measurements
5. **Preparations**: Multiple references to mixing (dal-related compounds)

The text is consistent with medieval descriptions of Vitis vinifera, which was classified variably by different authorities:
- Dioscorides: leaves are astringent (cooling), tendrils are used for dysentery
- Galen: wine is hot in 1st-2nd degree, grape juice is cold
- The MS text appears to describe multiple parts with different degree ratings, which matches this complexity
