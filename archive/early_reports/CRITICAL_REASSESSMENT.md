## FATAL FLAW #1: Frequency Mismatch (dalla is NOT #1)

**The attack**: "dalla" ranks ~50-100 in Italian, not #1. In pharmaceutical Italian, "di" (~3.5%) should be the most frequent word, not "dalla" (~0.3%).

**Assessment**: THIS IS VALID AND DEVASTATING.
- "daiin" frequency in Voynich: ~2.2% (rank #1)
- "dalla" frequency in Italian pharmaceutical texts: ~0.3-0.5% (rank ~40-60)
- Discrepancy: **5-7x too high**
- No known Italian text genre places "dalla" at rank #1

**Implication**: Either:
1. "daiin" does NOT mean "dalla"
2. The text is not standard Italian
3. The encoding is not a simple substitution cipher

## FATAL FLAW #2: s=/m/ is Phonetically Absurd

**The attack**: /s/ (voiceless alveolar fricative) and /m/ (voiced bilabial nasal) share ZERO articulatory features. This mapping contradicts the phonetic logic used for other mappings (i=/l/ from Hebrew lamed).

**Assessment**: THIS IS VALID.
- If the cipher has visual/phonetic logic: s should map to a sibilant
- If the cipher is purely arbitrary: the Hebrew lamed argument for i=/l/ is meaningless
- **The hypothesis is internally contradictory**

## FATAL FLAW #3: "facem" is a Latin Error

**The attack**: "facem" is the accusative of "fax" (torch), NOT of "facies" (face/appearance). The claimants confused two different Latin words.

**Assessment**: THIS IS VALID.
- "facies" (appearance) has accusative "faciem", NOT "facem"
- "facem" = torch/firebrand — irrelevant to a herbal opening
- **The "first word decoded" claim is based on a Latin error**
