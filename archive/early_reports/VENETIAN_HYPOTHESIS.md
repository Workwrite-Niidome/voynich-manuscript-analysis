## THE EVIDENCE CHAIN

1. **Consonant-final 58%** — Venetian systematically drops final vowels after continuants (n, l, r). Source: Wikipedia "Venetian language" + Orbis Latinus.

2. **-n/-l/-r each at ~26%** — Venetian drops vowels ONLY after n, l, r (in Venetian/Veronese dialect). This produces exactly the three-way equal distribution observed.

3. **The Carrara Herbal is Venetian** — The closest known parallel manuscript (BL Egerton 2020, Padua c.1400) is written in Paduan (= Venetian) dialect. Same time, same place, same language.

4. **"ol" = Venetian/Lombard article** — The independent fresh-eyes decoder (Cycle 1) identified "ol" as the Lombard definite article. Venetian uses "el" (similar).

5. **Trevisan vs Paduan = A/B split** — Trevisan drops vowels "very often" while Venetian/Veronese drops them "only after n, l, r." Two sub-dialects = two scribes with different conventions.

6. **Jewish community in Padua** — The University of Padua admitted Jewish students from the 15th century. Judeo-Venetian is a documented language variety.

7. **Historical plausibility** — A Jewish physician in Padua (c.1410-1430) writing pharmaceutical notes in Venetian dialect using Hebrew-cursive-derived script is entirely plausible.
