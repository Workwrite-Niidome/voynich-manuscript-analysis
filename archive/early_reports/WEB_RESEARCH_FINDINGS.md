## BREAKTHROUGH DISCOVERY: The Naibbe Cipher (2025)

**Paper**: Greshko, M. (2025). "The Naibbe cipher: a substitution cipher that encrypts Latin and Italian as Voynich Manuscript-like ciphertext." *Cryptologia*.

**Key finding**: A verbose homophonic substitution cipher using 6 tables + dice + playing cards can encrypt Italian/Latin text into Voynich-like output that reproduces many statistical properties.

**Relation to OUR work**:
- Our "nomenclator" conclusion and Greshko's "verbose homophonic substitution" are CONVERGENT
- Both identify Italian/Latin as the source language
- Both involve multiple substitution tables (we identified ch/sh categories; Greshko uses 6 tables)
- Both use 15th-century Italian materials
- **KEY DIFFERENCE**: Greshko doesn't claim to have decoded the manuscript; we go further in proposing the code/cipher separation

## CRITICAL COMPARISON TEXT: Carrara Herbal (c.1400)

**Location**: British Library, Egerton MS 2020
**Origin**: Padua, commissioned by Francesco II da Carrara (r. 1390-1405)
**Content**: Arabic pharmaceutical text (Serapion the Younger) translated into PADUAN DIALECT
**Status**: Digitized (images on Wikimedia Commons)

**Why this matters**:
- Same time period as Voynich (1390-1405 vs 1404-1438)
- Same location (Padua)
- Same content (pharmaceutical/herbal)
- Same language trajectory (Arabic medical knowledge → Northern Italian dialect)
- **This is the CLOSEST KNOWN PARALLEL to the Voynich Manuscript**
