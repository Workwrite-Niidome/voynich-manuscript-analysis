# Picatrix & Aurora Consurgens: Structural Parallels to the Voynich Manuscript

**Date**: 2026-04-04
**Purpose**: Evaluate whether the Voynich is better understood as a Picatrix-type magical-natural-philosophical compendium rather than a pure pharmaceutical herbal.

---

## 1. THE STRUCTURE OF THE PICATRIX (Ghayat al-Hakim)

### 1.1 Organization

The Picatrix is organized into **four Books** (Libri), each subdivided into chapters. The Latin translation (c.1256, from the Arabic original c.1000 CE) circulated widely in 15th-century Italy, particularly in Humanist circles.

| Book | Content | Chapters | Character |
|------|---------|----------|-----------|
| **Book I** | Cosmological theory: celestial spheres, planetary influences, theory of how celestial forces flow into matter | ~7 chapters | THEORETICAL — establishes the philosophical framework |
| **Book II** | Talismanic images and their construction: how to engrave images on stones/metals under proper celestial configurations | ~12 chapters | PRACTICAL-RITUAL — instructions for creating magical objects |
| **Book III** | Planetary correspondences: EXTENSIVE lists of plants, stones, animals, metals, colors, incenses associated with each of the 7 planets | ~12 chapters | MATERIA MAGICA — encyclopedic lists organized by planet |
| **Book IV** | Practical recipes: suffumigations (ritual incense burning), invocations, specific ritual procedures, planetary prayers | ~9 chapters | RECIPE/PROCEDURE — step-by-step instructions |

### 1.2 Voynich Section Mapping

| Voynich Section | Folios | Picatrix Equivalent | Match Quality |
|-----------------|--------|---------------------|---------------|
| **Herbal A** (f1r-f57v) | ~114 pages | Book III: Plants by planetary association | PARTIAL — Picatrix lists are tabular, not illustrated monographs |
| **Astronomical** (f67r-f73v) | ~14 pages | Book I: Celestial theory, planetary spheres | GOOD — both have circular diagrams with celestial content |
| **Biological/Balneological** (f75r-f84v) | ~20 pages | Book II: Talismanic procedures requiring bodily purification | WEAK — Picatrix has no bathing imagery |
| **Herbal B** (f87r-f102v) | ~32 pages | Book III continued: More materia medica | PARTIAL |
| **Recipe/Pharmaceutical** (f103r-f116v) | ~28 pages | Book IV: Suffumigations and practical recipes | GOOD — both have short recipe-format entries |

### 1.3 Critical Differences

**AGAINST the Picatrix parallel:**
1. The Picatrix has NO plant illustrations. Its materia medica sections are pure TEXT LISTS. The Voynich devotes enormous space to individual plant drawings.
2. The Picatrix has NO nude figures or bathing scenes. Its procedures are ritual, not bodily.
3. The Picatrix's planetary-plant lists are organized by PLANET (all Saturn plants together, all Jupiter plants together). If the Voynich herbal sections were Picatrix-type, we would expect ~7 groups of plants. Instead, the herbal sections show no clear 7-fold division.
4. The Picatrix's recipe sections contain INVOCATIONS and PRAYERS interspersed with material recipes. These would require distinctive textual markers (divine names, repeated formulae). Our statistical analysis of the Voynich recipe section (f103r-f116v) shows no such repeated invocation patterns.
5. The Picatrix is a LONG text (4 books, hundreds of pages in most manuscripts). The Voynich at ~240 pages is consistent with a herbal compendium but short for a complete Picatrix copy.

**FOR the Picatrix parallel:**
1. The multi-section structure (theory + materia medica + recipes) matches better than a single-purpose herbal.
2. The astronomical section fits naturally as "timing instructions" for operations.
3. The combination of plants + stars + procedures is characteristic of astrological medicine / magical naturalism, not pure pharmacy.
4. The encryption motive is stronger for a banned magical text than for a simple herbal.

## 2. AURORA CONSURGENS: THE NUDE FIGURES IN WATER

### 2.1 What the Aurora Consurgens Contains

The Aurora Consurgens (Zurich ZB Rh. 172, c.1420, South German-North Italian provenance) is an alchemical treatise attributed (probably falsely) to Thomas Aquinas. It exists in two parts:

- **Part I**: A theological-alchemical text full of Biblical quotations reinterpreted as alchemical allegory
- **Part II**: Practical alchemical recipes (the "recipe" section)

The manuscript is richly illustrated with 38 miniatures depicting:
- **Nude and semi-nude figures** in various poses
- **Couples in baths/vessels** representing conjunction (coniunctio)
- **Celestial symbols** (sun, moon, stars) above the figures
- **Plants and minerals** associated with alchemical substances
- **Colored liquids** in which figures are immersed

### 2.2 What the Nude Figures Represent in Alchemy

The nude figures in water in the Aurora Consurgens represent specific alchemical concepts:

| Image | Alchemical Meaning | Technical Term |
|-------|-------------------|----------------|
| **Woman in bath** | Mercury (philosophical mercury) undergoing purification | Ablutio (washing) |
| **Man and woman together in vessel** | Union of sulfur and mercury; conjunction of opposites | Coniunctio |
| **Figure dissolving in water** | Dissolution of the base material | Solutio |
| **Figures emerging from bath** | Purified substance after treatment | Coagulatio / Resurrectio |
| **Crowned figures after bathing** | The perfected substance (philosophical gold) | Rubedo / Lapis |
| **Multiple figures in connected pools** | Sequential stages of purification | Circulatio |

The water/bath imagery is THE central metaphor of Western alchemy: the alchemical "bath" (balneum) is both literal (water baths used in alchemical heating) and symbolic (purification of matter through dissolution).

### 2.3 Comparison with Voynich Biological Section (f75r-f84v)

| Feature | Aurora Consurgens | Voynich Biological Section |
|---------|-------------------|---------------------------|
| **Nude figures** | Yes, male and female | Yes, predominantly female ("nymphs") |
| **Immersed in water/liquid** | Yes, colored baths | Yes, green/blue liquid flows |
| **Connected vessels/pools** | Yes, linked by tubes | Yes, elaborate plumbing-like connections |
| **Celestial symbols above** | Yes, sun/moon/stars | Yes (some folios show stars) |
| **Figures in groups** | Yes, pairs primarily | Yes, groups of 3-10+ |
| **Body types** | Naturalistic, some idealized | Stylized, similar body types |
| **Gender** | Both male and female | Almost exclusively female |
| **Coupled pairs** | Central motif (coniunctio) | Absent — no male-female coupling |
| **Biblical/text integration** | Heavy — figures illustrate text passages | Minimal text near figures |
| **Artistic style** | Professional illumination | Naive/folk style |

### 2.4 Are the Voynich Nymphs the Same as Aurora Consurgens Figures?

**Partially yes, but with critical differences:**

**Similarities that suggest shared tradition:**
- Connected pools/vessels with figures inside them — this is a distinctive motif
- The "plumbing" connecting pools in the Voynich is strikingly similar to alchemical apparatus diagrams (pelicans, alembics, circulatory vessels)
- The green/blue color of the liquid matches alchemical "viriditas" (the green force of nature, central to Paracelsian and pre-Paracelsian alchemy)

**Differences that argue against direct connection:**
- The Aurora Consurgens emphasizes MALE-FEMALE PAIRS (the coniunctio of opposites). The Voynich has almost exclusively female figures. This is a fundamental conceptual difference.
- In alchemy, the bath scene ALWAYS involves transformation: figures go IN one state and come OUT another (often shown by color change). The Voynich shows no such before/after transformation.
- Alchemical bath imagery is paired with specific textual references (often Biblical). The Voynich biological section has relatively sparse text.

**Alternative interpretation:**
The Voynich biological section may represent **medical/gynecological bathing practices** rather than alchemical allegory. 15th-century Italian medical texts frequently prescribed therapeutic baths (bagni), especially for women's health conditions. The Paduan medical tradition had extensive balneological literature (De Balneis, compiled at Padua). The connected pools could represent sequential bath treatments at different temperatures or with different herbal infusions — a literal medical practice, not an alchemical metaphor.

## 3. IF THE VOYNICH IS A PICATRIX-TYPE TEXT: WHAT CHANGES?

### 3.1 Reinterpretation of Sections

| Current Interpretation | Picatrix Interpretation | Impact on Decipherment |
|-----------------------|------------------------|----------------------|
| "Plants" = botanical descriptions | "Plants" = astrological-botanical (each plant tagged to a planet) | MAJOR — would expect planetary markers (7 categories) in herbal text |
| "Recipes" = pharmaceutical preparations | "Recipes" = magical suffumigations/incantations | MODERATE — recipe STRUCTURE is similar, but vocabulary differs |
| "Bathing" = medical balneology | "Bathing" = ritual purification before magical operations | MINOR — changes interpretation but not text structure |
| "Astronomy" = calendar/timekeeping | "Astronomy" = timing instructions for magical operations | MINIMAL — same kind of content either way |

### 3.2 What WOULD Change in Our Statistical Findings

**Things that REMAIN VALID regardless of herbal vs. magical interpretation:**
- The nomenclator structure (65% code / 35% cipher) — applies to ANY content
- The Italian grammatical skeleton (di, e, con, il) — applies to ANY Italian text
- The two-scribe identification — applies regardless
- The gallows-initial paragraph marking — applies regardless

**Things that WOULD change:**
1. **The "ana" = aiin mapping becomes WEAKER.** "Ana" (of each, equal parts) is specifically pharmaceutical. If the recipes are suffumigations, the Picatrix uses different dosage language: "take [amount] of [substance] and burn it at the hour of [planet]". The word "ana" appears rarely if ever in Picatrix-type recipes.

2. **The code-word categories might shift.** If ch- words are not plants but planetary-plant associations, and sh- words are not preparations but ritual actions, the codebook would need to be organized differently.

3. **We would expect PLANETARY NAMES or abbreviations.** In the Picatrix, the seven planets (Saturn, Jupiter, Mars, Sun, Venus, Mercury, Moon) are constantly referenced. If the Voynich is Picatrix-type, we should find 7 very-high-frequency code words that appear throughout all sections. Do we? Let me check against our frequency data:

Our top code words: chol, shol, okaiin, chedy, otaiin, shed, oky, shy, chey, shaiin. That is NOT 7 words — and the prefix structure (ch-, sh-, ok-, ot-) does not suggest a 7-fold division.

**This is a significant argument AGAINST the Picatrix interpretation.**

## 4. PICATRIX vs. PHARMACEUTICAL VOCABULARY OVERLAP

### 4.1 Where They Overlap

The Picatrix's Book III (materia medica) shares approximately 60-70% of its plant/mineral vocabulary with standard pharmaceutical texts. This is because:
- Both traditions derive from the same Arabic sources (Dioscorides via Arabic translation, Serapion, Avicenna)
- Plants like rue (ruta), hellebore (elleboro), pepper (pepe), myrrh (mirra) appear in BOTH magical and pharmaceutical lists
- Mineral substances (sulfur, mercury, lead, copper) appear in both

### 4.2 Where They Diverge

| Feature | Picatrix Vocabulary | Pharmaceutical Vocabulary |
|---------|-------------------|--------------------------|
| **Organizing principle** | By PLANET (7 categories) | By AILMENT or ALPHABETICAL |
| **Dosage language** | "at the hour of Mars" + "burn on coals" | "ana drams 3" + "boil in water" |
| **Action verbs** | "engrave", "suffumigate", "invoke", "seal" | "grind", "boil", "mix", "strain", "apply" |
| **Temporal markers** | Planetary hours, lunar mansions, zodiac degrees | "for 3 days", "every morning", "until healed" |
| **Addresses/invocations** | "O spirit of Saturn..." (prayer formulae) | None |
| **Body references** | Rare (except in medical-magical crossover) | Constant (ailments, body parts, symptoms) |
| **Measurement units** | Weight-based but less precise | drams, scruples, ounces — highly systematized |

### 4.3 Distinguishing Test

If we could decode even a few more Voynich words, we could definitively distinguish:
- Finding "ora" (hour) or "pianeta" (planet) = Picatrix-type
- Finding "bollire" (boil) or "pestare" (grind) = pharmaceutical
- Finding "suffumiga" (fumigate) = could be either (fumigations exist in pharmacy too, but are central to Picatrix)
- Finding body part names (testa, occhi, orecchie) = pharmaceutical (treating ailments)

**Our existing evidence points pharmaceutical:** The recipe section's structure (282 entries, each starting with a different word = plant/condition name, followed by ingredient lists) is the format of a medical recipe book, not a magical grimoire.

## 5. THE BANNED TEXT HYPOTHESIS

### 5.1 The Picatrix's Status

The Picatrix was indeed problematic for the Church:
- It was never formally placed on the Index Librorum Prohibitorum (which was created in 1559, after the Voynich's likely composition date)
- However, it was clearly understood as DEMONIC MAGIC by Church authorities — it contains invocations to planetary spirits that were classified as demons
- Alfonso X of Castile commissioned the Latin translation (c.1256) despite ecclesiastical opposition
- Copies circulated semi-clandestinely in 15th-century Humanist circles
- Marsilio Ficino (Florence, 1470s-90s) carefully distanced his own astrological-magical works from the Picatrix's more explicit demonic content

### 5.2 Would Encryption Be Justified?

**FOR the banned-text hypothesis:**
- Possessing a text containing demonic invocations was genuinely dangerous in 15th-century Italy
- The Inquisition was active in Northern Italy throughout the 15th century
- Several documented cases of scholars prosecuted for possessing magical texts
- A Jewish physician (fitting our existing hypothesis) would be doubly vulnerable — Jewish identity + forbidden magical knowledge
- The Voynich's encryption is EXPENSIVE (nomenclator construction, consistent encoding across 240 pages). This level of effort suggests the content was genuinely dangerous, not merely private

**AGAINST:**
- Simple pharmaceutical herbals were NOT dangerous. There was no reason to encrypt a recipe for cough medicine.
- BUT: The Voynich might contain BOTH innocent pharmaceutical content AND dangerous magical/alchemical content. The encryption protects the whole text to conceal the dangerous parts.
- Counter-argument: If only some sections were dangerous, why not encrypt only those? The consistent encryption of ALL sections suggests either ALL content was sensitive, or the encryption serves a different purpose (trade secrecy, intellectual property).

### 5.3 Trade Secret Hypothesis (Alternative to Banned Text)

A pharmaceutical compendium could justify encryption if it represented:
- A physician's proprietary recipe collection (his competitive advantage)
- Guild secrets (apothecary guilds guarded formulae)
- A compilation of expensive/rare recipes gathered over a career

This is actually a STRONGER motivation for consistent encryption than a banned text, because:
- ALL the content would be commercially valuable (explaining why ALL sections are encrypted)
- The owner would need to ACCESS the text regularly (explaining why the encryption is systematic, not ad hoc)
- Trade secrets in pharmacy were a documented practice in 15th-century Italy

### 5.4 Hybrid Possibility

The most likely scenario, considering all evidence:

**The Voynich is primarily a pharmaceutical compendium (explaining 80% of its content) that MAY include some astrological-medical content (the astronomical section) reflecting the standard 15th-century practice of timing medical treatments by celestial configurations.**

This is NOT the same as the Picatrix. It is standard Galenic-Arabic medicine as practiced in Northern Italy:
- Treat ailments with herbal preparations (herbal sections)
- Time treatments by planetary hours (astronomical section)
- Prepare the patient's body through bathing/purification (biological section)
- Follow specific recipes (recipe section)

This is entirely orthodox medicine, not banned magic. The encryption is more likely motivated by TRADE SECRECY than by fear of the Inquisition.

## 6. SYNTHESIS: DOES THE PICATRIX PARALLEL HOLD?

### Verdict: WEAK PARALLEL — The pharmaceutical herbal interpretation remains superior.

| Criterion | Picatrix Score | Pharmaceutical Score | Winner |
|-----------|---------------|---------------------|--------|
| Multi-section structure | 7/10 | 8/10 | PHARMA (herbals also have multiple sections) |
| Plant illustrations | 2/10 | 9/10 | PHARMA (Picatrix has no plant illustrations) |
| Recipe format | 5/10 | 9/10 | PHARMA (recipe structure matches herbal format) |
| Astronomical section | 8/10 | 6/10 | PICATRIX (planetary timing fits magical operations better) |
| Biological/bathing section | 4/10 | 7/10 | PHARMA (medical bathing more likely than ritual purification) |
| Vocabulary evidence | 3/10 | 8/10 | PHARMA (ana, olio, di match pharmaceutical not magical) |
| Encryption motive | 7/10 | 6/10 | SLIGHT PICATRIX (banned text explains encryption well) |
| Regional context | 6/10 | 9/10 | PHARMA (Carrara Herbal is much closer parallel) |
| **TOTAL** | **42/80** | **62/80** | **PHARMACEUTICAL** |

### What the Picatrix Comparison DOES Teach Us

1. **The astronomical section may encode planetary timing for medical operations** — this is standard Galenic practice, not magic, but the Picatrix comparison helps us think about it correctly.

2. **The bathing section could involve preparatory purification** — whether medical (preparing the patient's body) or ritual (preparing the practitioner), the function is similar.

3. **The encryption is better explained by trade secrecy than by banned-text fear** — but we should not entirely rule out that some content (e.g., the astronomical section) touches on astrological practices that could attract unwanted attention.

4. **The Aurora Consurgens nude-in-water imagery is the closest visual parallel to the Voynich biological section among known manuscripts** — even if the meaning differs (alchemical vs. medical), the VISUAL TRADITION of depicting figures in connected water vessels appears to be shared.

### Actionable Next Steps

1. **Section-specific word frequency analysis**: Count aiin, ol, and other mapped words PER SECTION. If aiin is pharmaceutical ("ana"), it should spike in recipe sections. If it is constant across all sections, it is more likely a general function word (weakening pharmaceutical interpretation).

2. **7-fold structure test**: Check if the herbal sections show any grouping into 7 categories (planetary association). Count the number of distinct "first words" or section markers. If there are ~7 groups, the Picatrix parallel strengthens.

3. **Aurora Consurgens visual comparison**: Compare the specific "plumbing" connecting pools in the Voynich biological section (f75r-f84v) with the apparatus diagrams in Aurora Consurgens and other alchemical manuscripts. If the connection patterns match alchemical distillation apparatus, the alchemical interpretation gains ground.

4. **Temporal/planetary vocabulary search**: Look for any Voynich word that appears ONLY in the astronomical section AND in the recipe section (but NOT in herbal sections). Such a word could be a planetary name or timing term.
