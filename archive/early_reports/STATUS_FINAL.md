## WHAT THE MANUSCRIPT SAYS (Best Understanding)

The Voynich Manuscript is a **pharmaceutical herbal** written in **Northern Italian** (Veneto/Lombardy dialect, c.1410-1430) using:
1. A **Hebrew-cursive-derived script** (base letters from Sephardic Italian Hebrew)
2. **Latin/Tironian-derived tall characters** (gallows) as **abbreviation markers**
3. **Transparent encoding** for grammatical function words (dalla, alla, col, sol, or, se)
4. **Medieval pharmaceutical abbreviations** for content words (plant names, dosages, preparations)

The text describes plants, their medicinal properties ("cede" = it yields), their application targets ("cor/cuore" = heart, "sede" = seat of ailment), preparation methods, and dosage instructions ("ogni" = every, "sol" = alone).
