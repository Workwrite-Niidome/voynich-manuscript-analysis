# Voynich Manuscript -- Botanical Illustration Plant Identification

## Methodology

Each folio was examined for: petal count, leaf shape (margins, venation, arrangement),
stem structure, root morphology, flower shape/color, and overall habit. Candidates were
drawn from plants commonly depicted in 15th-century North Italian herbals (e.g.,
*Tractatus de Herbis*, Carrara Herbal, Serapion the Younger, Platearius *Circa Instans*).

Confidence levels: HIGH (>60%), MODERATE (30--60%), LOW (<30%).

## 1. f1r (page 3) -- First Herbal Entry

### Illustration Description
- **Habit**: Medium-sized plant, upright, branching from a single stem
- **Leaves**: Ovate to elliptical, entire margins (smooth, no serration), alternate arrangement, approximately 15--20 leaves visible. Leaves painted in two tones -- green and yellow/ochre -- possibly indicating different stages or upper/lower surfaces
- **Stem**: Single central stem, relatively thick, light-colored
- **Flower**: A single terminal structure at the apex, brownish/dull, appears to be a capitulum or bud rather than open petals
- **Root**: A dense, fibrous/matted root mass drawn as cross-hatching at the base -- suggests a taproot or thick rhizome
- **Text**: Extensive Voynichese text both above and below the illustration in two columns

### Candidate Identifications

| # | Plant | Reasoning | Confidence |
|---|-------|-----------|------------|
| 1 | **Laurus nobilis** (bay laurel) | Ovate entire leaves, alternate arrangement, single terminal structure, woody stem. Bay laurel was one of the most important pharmaceutical plants in medieval Italy. The two-tone leaf coloring could represent the lighter underside. | MODERATE (45%) |
| 2 | **Myrtus communis** (myrtle) | Similar leaf shape, Mediterranean plant, extensively used in medieval pharmacy | MODERATE (30%) |
| 3 | **Pistacia lentiscus** (mastic) | Ovate leaves, alternate, important in medieval North Italian pharmacy for resins | LOW (20%) |
| 4 | **Punica granatum** (pomegranate) | Ovate entire leaves, prominent in herbals, but usually depicted with fruit | LOW (15%) |

### Best Match for 15th-century North Italian Use
**Laurus nobilis** -- Bay laurel was foundational to medieval Italian pharmacy. It appears in virtually every herbal of the period. The depiction style (bicolor leaves, single stem, matted root) is consistent with the simplified Carrara Herbal style. If this is indeed *Laurus*, the opening Voynichese word on f1r could encode "lauro" or "alloro" (Italian for bay laurel).

## 9. f47r (page 93) -- Two Enormous Lobed Leaves

### Illustration Description
- **Habit**: NOT a full plant -- TWO isolated enormous leaves displayed on the page, one above the other
- **Upper leaf**: Palmately lobed, approximately 5 lobes, rounded margins, deep green. Slightly smaller than the lower leaf
- **Lower leaf**: Very large, palmately lobed, 5--7 rounded lobes, deep green, taking up nearly half the page
- **Both leaves**: Rounded lobe tips (not pointed), sinuate margins, palmate venation implied by lobe arrangement
- **Root**: Very small, barely visible at the bottom -- a single thin root with a small reddish termination
- **Marginal decoration**: Small red figures/marks in the right margin
- **Text**: Two blocks of text between the leaves, with the Voynichese script
- **Folio number**: "47" written at top right

### Candidate Identifications

| # | Plant | Reasoning | Confidence |
|---|-------|-----------|------------|
| 1 | **Vitis vinifera** (grape vine) | Palmately lobed leaves with rounded lobes is DIAGNOSTIC for grape. The 5-lobed structure with sinuate/rounded margins matches grape leaves almost exactly. Grape was the single most important cultivated plant in medieval Italy. The depiction of leaves alone (without fruit) is consistent with an herbal focusing on the pharmaceutical properties of vine leaves | HIGH (70%) |
| 2 | **Ficus carica** (fig) | Palmately lobed, but fig lobes are typically deeper and fewer (3--5) | MODERATE (25%) |
| 3 | **Acer** sp. (maple) | Palmately lobed, but lobes are usually pointed, not rounded | LOW (10%) |
| 4 | **Platanus** sp. (plane tree) | Palmately lobed, but lobes are more pointed | LOW (10%) |
| 5 | **Ribes** sp. (currant) | Small palmately lobed leaves, but too small in reality | LOW (5%) |

### Best Match
**Vitis vinifera (grape vine)** -- This is the HIGHEST confidence identification in this study. The 5--7 rounded palmate lobes with sinuate margins are essentially unmistakable grape vine leaves. In medieval Italian pharmacy, vine leaves ("foglie di vite") were used medicinally for diarrhea, hemorrhage, and inflammation. The grape vine was depicted in every major medieval Italian herbal.

**Critical note for decipherment**: The text between these two grape leaves should contain the Voynichese encoding of "vitis" / "vite" / "uva" (Latin/Italian for grape vine). Prior analysis noted "chol chol chol" repetitions on this page. If "chol" = "foglia/folia" (leaf), then the text may read as a description of grape leaves. Alternatively, "chol" could encode "vite" itself.

## Note on Limitations

The Voynich botanical illustrations are notoriously stylized and sometimes appear to combine features from multiple plants or include fantastical elements. The identifications above are based on the most diagnostic morphological features visible in the illustrations, compared with how these same plants were typically depicted in contemporary Italian herbals (not how they appear in nature). Some illustrations may represent plants that no longer exist in the same form, regional varieties, or composite/imaginary plants.
