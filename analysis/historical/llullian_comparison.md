## 1. Llull's Ars Magna: Structure and Mechanism

### 1.1 The Core Principle

Ramon Llull (1232-1316) created a system for generating all valid propositions about a domain by:
1. Assigning **single letters** (B through K) to **fundamental categories**
2. **Combining** these letters mechanically through rotating wheels
3. **Reading off** the combinations as meaningful statements

The system was explicitly designed to be **exhaustive**: every valid combination of categories should be generated and examined.

### 1.2 The Alphabet of the Art

Each letter carries **six simultaneous meanings** depending on context:

| Letter | Attribute | Relation | Question | Subject | Virtue | Vice |
|--------|-----------|----------|----------|---------|--------|------|
| B | Goodness | Difference | Whether? | God | Justice | Avarice |
| C | Greatness | Concordance | What? | Angels | Prudence | Gluttony |
| D | Duration | Contrariety | Of what? | Heaven | Fortitude | Lust |
| E | Power | Beginning | Why? | Man | Temperance | Conceit |
| F | Wisdom | Middle | How much? | Imagination | Faith | Sloth |
| G | Will | End | What quality? | Senses | Hope | Envy |
| H | Virtue | Majority | When? | Vegetation | Charity | Wrath |
| I | Truth | Equality | Where? | Elements | Patience | Lies |
| K | Glory | Minority | How/with what? | Instruments | Compassion | Inconstancy |

**Key insight**: A single letter encodes an entire **column** of related concepts. The letter is not a phoneme or a word -- it is a **categorical index**. This is exactly how the Voynich's prefixes appear to function.

Source: [Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy - Ramon Llull](https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/llull/)

### 1.3 The Four Figures

**First Figure**: A single circle divided into 9 sectors (B-K), each labeled with divine attributes. Establishes the vocabulary.

**Second Figure**: Three triangles inscribed in a circle, encoding 9 relational principles (difference/concordance/contrariety; beginning/middle/end; majority/equality/minority).

**Third Figure**: A square table of all **binary** combinations (BC, BD, BE... IK). Generates 36 unique pairs from 9 elements.

**Fourth Figure**: **Three concentric rotating circles**, each bearing the letters B-K. By rotating the circles independently, all **ternary combinations** are generated. Starting from BCB, rotating produces CCB, then BDB, systematically generating 252 distinct triads.

**The Fourth Figure is the structural key.** It combines exactly 3 elements at a time, producing a systematically generated vocabulary of combinations.

Source: [Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy - Ramon Llull](https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/llull/)

### 1.4 How Combinations Generate Knowledge

The process called "evacuation" extracts meaning from combinations:
- For combination BC (Goodness-Greatness), the system generates 12 affirmations through permutation
- Each affirmation spawns 3 questions (whether, what, what quality)
- Result: 36 propositions from a single pair
- For ternary combination BCD: 20 questions are generated

The system is designed to be **productive** -- every possible combination yields meaningful output, and **no valid combination is excluded**.

### 2.2 The Ternary Structure Match

Llull's **Fourth Figure** is the most complex, combining **exactly 3 elements** through three concentric circles. The Voynich word structure is **exactly ternary**:

```
LLULL'S FOURTH FIGURE:     Outer Circle  +  Middle Circle  +  Inner Circle
                           (1st letter)     (2nd letter)      (3rd letter)
                           Example: B    +  C             +  D

VOYNICH WORD:              PREFIX        +  ROOT          +  SUFFIX
                           (category)       (specific item)   (modification)
                           Example: ch-  +  ol            +  -dy
```

The parallel is not superficial. Both systems:
1. Define a **finite set of elements** for each position
2. **Mechanically combine** them to generate all valid outputs
3. Achieve **near-complete paradigm coverage** (every element in position 1 can combine with every element in position 2)
4. Are designed to be **exhaustive** rather than expressive

### 2.3 Paradigm Completeness: The Smoking Gun

The Voynich's paradigm completeness (87.3%) is the single strongest piece of evidence for a Llullian-type system:

| System | Paradigm Completeness | Interpretation |
|--------|----------------------|----------------|
| Natural languages | 30-60% | Lexical gaps from historical accidents |
| **Voynich** | **87.3%** | Designed combinatorial coverage |
| Llull's Art | 100% (by design) | Every valid combination is generated |
| Agglutinative languages (Turkish) | 60-75% | High but not exhaustive |

The top 5 Voynich prefixes (ch-, d-, sh-, o-, y-) achieve **100% completeness** with the 15 most common roots. This is not a natural-language phenomenon -- it is the signature of a designed system where "general categories accept everything."

The less frequent prefixes (qok-, ot-, ok-) achieve 73-80%, suggesting they are **specialized categories** with restricted applicability -- exactly what a pharmaceutical notation would require (not every preparation method applies to every substance).

### 2.4 What a "Written Llullian Wheel" Would Look Like

If someone wrote down all the outputs of a Llullian wheel system instead of reading them from rotating discs, the result would be:

1. **Words of regular structure**: PREFIX+ROOT+SUFFIX, always in the same order
2. **High paradigm completeness**: Most combinations would be attested
3. **Category-dependent suffix distributions**: Not all suffixes would apply to all prefix categories (just as Llull's Fourth Figure generates valid and invalid combinations that must be filtered)
4. **A vocabulary that looks constructed**: Regular morphology, clean segmentability, no irregular forms
5. **Content organized by categories**: Pages would cluster by topic (all plants of one type together)

**The Voynich matches all five predictions.**

## 3. Llull's Medical Works: The Missing Link

### 3.1 Llull Applied His Art to Medicine

Llull himself wrote medical works applying his combinatorial method:

- **Ars compendiosa medicinae** (Compendious Art of Medicine) -- applies the combinatorial Art to medical diagnosis and treatment
- **Liber de regionibus sanitatis et infirmitatis** (Book of the Regions of Health and Illness, op. 107) -- systematic classification of health conditions

These works demonstrate that **Llull himself saw medicine as a domain for combinatorial treatment**. His Quaternary Phase (organized around the four elements: earth, water, air, fire) was directly compatible with Galenic humoral theory.

Sources: [Ghent University Library](https://lib.ugent.be/en/catalog/ebk01:3790000000128223), [Google Books - Opera medica](https://books.google.com/books/about/Opera_medica_continens_quatuor_libros.html?hl=en&id=TGgOGwAACAAJ&output=html_text)

### 3.2 The Galenic Degree System: Already Combinatorial

Medieval pharmaceutical classification was inherently combinatorial:

| Quality | Degree 1 (mild) | Degree 2 | Degree 3 | Degree 4 (extreme) |
|---------|-----------------|----------|----------|---------------------|
| Hot | Borage | Fennel | Angelica | Garlic |
| Cold | Lettuce | Nightshade | Hemlock | Opium |
| Wet | Cucumber | Melon | - | - |
| Dry | Sage | Myrrh | Pepper | - |

Every herb was classified by **2 qualities x 4 degrees = 8 possible positions**. This is already a combinatorial matrix. A Llullian practitioner would naturally extend this:

```
HERB DESCRIPTION = QUALITY + SUBSTANCE + PREPARATION
                 = [hot/cold/wet/dry] + [specific plant] + [form: powder/tincture/decoction]
```

This maps directly to: PREFIX + ROOT + SUFFIX.

### 3.3 Arnold of Villanova: The Bridge Between Llull and Pharmacy

Arnold of Villanova (c.1240-1311) was:
- A contemporary and probable acquaintance of Llull (they likely met in Marseilles)
- The most celebrated medical teacher at Montpellier for a decade
- Physician to four kings and three popes
- Author of ~70 medical texts integrating Arabic, Jewish, and European traditions
- A practitioner of medical alchemy, with numerous texts falsely attributed to him

Arnold represents the **direct connection** between Llullian combinatorial thinking and pharmaceutical practice. Both men were Catalan, both were active in the same intellectual networks, and both had followers who produced pseudo-epigraphic works (false attributions) well into the 15th century.

Source: [Encyclopedia.com - Arnau de Villanova](https://www.encyclopedia.com/science/encyclopedias-almanacs-transcripts-and-maps/arnau-de-villanova)

### 3.4 John of Rupescissa: Pharmaceutical Alchemy's Systematic Approach

John of Rupescissa (c.1310-c.1366), a Franciscan friar, has been credited with inventing "medical chemistry" -- the conceptual forerunner of pharmacology. His key innovation:

- Proposed that a **quinta essentia** (fifth essence) could be distilled from different herbs, animal products, and minerals
- Argued that different quintessences produce **different internal medicines suited to different diseases**
- Created a **systematic framework** integrating empirical distillation with Aristotelian cosmology

Rupescissa's approach was fundamentally **classificatory**: substance X distilled by process Y yields medicine Z for disease W. This is a combinatorial structure that begs for systematic notation.

Source: [Cambridge Core - Medical Alchemy and Quintessence](https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/bjhs-themes/article/medical-alchemy-and-quintessence-in-renaissance-florence)

## 4. Pseudo-Lullian Alchemy in 15th-Century Italy

### 4.1 The Corpus

A massive corpus of **143 alchemical texts** circulated under Llull's name, none authentic. The most important:

- **Testamentum** (c.1332): The earliest pseudo-Lullian alchemical work, likely by an anonymous Catalan scholar ("Magister Testamentum") who studied medicine at Montpellier
- **Liber de secretis naturae seu de quinta essentia**: Contains the *Tractatus septem rotarum* (Treatise of Seven Wheels, of which six rotate) -- explicitly using Llullian rotating wheel diagrams for alchemical processes
- **Codicillus**: Further development of the Testamentum's system

### 4.2 Italian Concentration

The pseudo-Lullian tradition was **overwhelmingly concentrated in Italy**:

- Of 13 known manuscripts of the *Testamentum novissimum*, **all but one are in Italy**
- Manuscripts exist in **Padua** (Biblioteca Universitaria 2087) and **Milan** (Archivio di Sant'Ambrogio)
- A key manuscript of the *Liber de secretis naturae* was copied in **Venice** on October 8, 1498, "in the house of Leonardo Loredano" (future Doge of Venice), by the scribe Johannes Cycurius Theatinus
- A Lullian school in Barcelona produced academics who taught in **Bologna, Venice, and Padua**
- Christophorus Parisiensis, likely from Venice, was an enthusiastic follower of pseudo-Lullian alchemy in the second half of the 15th century

Sources: [TextManuscripts - Pseudo-Lull Testamentum](https://www.textmanuscripts.com/medieval/lull-theorica-testamentum-61038), [TextManuscripts - Liber de secretis naturae](https://www.textmanuscripts.com/medieval/liber-secretis-naturae-lull-60945)

### 4.3 The Diagrams: Wheels, Alphabets, and Tables

The pseudo-Lullian alchemical tradition used:

1. **Alphabets where each letter corresponds to one alchemical principle** -- directly derived from Llull's B-K letter system
2. **Circular figures** (figura S, figura individuorum) -- colored in red, yellow, and green, representing different aspects of alchemical processes
3. **Tables arranging letters** so that practitioners could "discover (invenire) the true alchemical processes" -- the same combinatorial principle as Llull's Third and Fourth Figures
4. **The arbor philosophicalis** (philosophical tree) -- a hierarchical classification of substances
5. **Seven rotating wheels** (septem rotarum quarum sex sunt volubiles) -- explicit Llullian wheel diagrams applied to chemistry

The entire corpus was cast into "tables, circular diagrams, and geometric figures, such as the square, the triangle, and letter symbolism."

Source: [Wikipedia - Lullism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lullism)

### 4.4 The Testamentum's Medical Recipes

The Testamentum includes approximately **50 medical recipes** following the theoretical text, including treatments for paralysis ("Contra paralysym vngumentum") and kidney stones. This demonstrates that the pseudo-Lullian tradition was not purely about transmutation -- it included **practical pharmaceutical content**.

A text that uses Llullian combinatorial alphabets AND contains pharmaceutical recipes is exactly the kind of intellectual ancestor that could have produced the Voynich's notation system.

## 5. The Voynich's Circular Diagrams and Llullian Wheels

### 5.1 The Voynich's Circular Imagery

The Voynich manuscript contains extensive circular diagrams:

- **Cosmological section** (f67r-f73v): 14 pages of circular diagrams featuring Sun, Moon, and stars
- **Rosettes foldout** (f85v-f86r): A massive 6-page foldout with 9 circular "rosettes" connected by causeways, containing castles and possibly a volcano
- **Zodiac pages**: Circular arrangements of zodiac figures with text

### 5.2 Comparison with Llullian Wheels

| Feature | Llullian Wheels | Voynich Circular Diagrams |
|---------|----------------|--------------------------|
| **Concentric circles** | Yes (2-3 rotating circles) | Yes (multiple concentric rings) |
| **Sectors with labels** | Yes (B-K in each sector) | Yes (words/labels in sectors) |
| **Rotational principle** | Yes (circles rotate independently) | Unknown (static drawings) |
| **Number of elements** | 9 per circle | Varies (12 in zodiac = months, 9 in rosettes) |
| **Connecting structures** | Triangles linking sectors | Causeways connecting rosettes |
| **Color coding** | Red, yellow, green in pseudo-Lullian | Green, blue, red in Voynich |

### 5.3 The Nine Rosettes

The Voynich's famous foldout features **9 circular structures**. Llull's system centers on **9 Dignities** (B-K). The number 9 appears in both systems:

- Llull: 9 attributes per figure, 9 principles, 9 questions
- Voynich foldout: 9 rosettes connected in a network

This could be coincidence, but in a manuscript already showing Llullian structural features in its word-formation, the numerical correspondence deserves attention.

### 5.4 Key Difference

Llullian wheels are **functional tools** -- you rotate them to generate combinations. The Voynich's circular diagrams appear to be **illustrative/cosmological** rather than combinatorial tools. However, if the Voynich is a **written record** of a wheel-based system (rather than the wheels themselves), the circular diagrams might represent the **conceptual framework** behind the notation -- showing the reader what categories exist and how they relate, while the text provides the actual combinatorial output.

## 6. The "Written Wheel" Hypothesis

### 6.1 The Core Proposal

**The Voynich manuscript is a written implementation of a Llullian combinatorial system applied to pharmaceutical knowledge.**

Instead of rotating physical wheels to generate combinations, the author:
1. Designed a morpheme-based notation (prefixes = outer wheel categories, roots = middle wheel substances, suffixes = inner wheel modifications)
2. Wrote out the relevant combinations as text
3. Organized the text by topic (herbal, astronomical, recipe sections)
4. Included circular diagrams showing the conceptual categories (the "wheels" behind the notation)

### 6.2 How the System Would Work

```
OUTER WHEEL (PREFIX):  ~15 categories
  ch- = plant type A (e.g., hot herbs?)
  sh- = plant type B (e.g., cold herbs?)
  d-  = preparation method?
  o-  = body system/application?
  qo- = compound category (restricted use)

MIDDLE WHEEL (ROOT):  ~200-400 specific items
  -ol-  = specific substance (oil? olive?)
  -ain- = specific substance
  -ed-  = specific substance
  [derived from abbreviated real names]

INNER WHEEL (SUFFIX):  ~7-10 modifications
  -y   = form A (dry/powder?)
  -n   = form B (liquid?)
  -dy  = compound modifier
  -aiin = compound modifier
  -r   = form C
```

A word like **cholaiin** would decode as:
- ch- (category: hot herb) + ol (specific substance) + -aiin (preparation: decoction?)
- = "decoction of [substance ol] from hot herb category"

### 6.3 Why This Explains the Voynich's Properties

| Voynich Property | Explanation Under Written-Wheel Hypothesis |
|-----------------|-------------------------------------------|
| 87.3% paradigm completeness | Designed combinatorial system -- every valid combination is generated |
| 78.4% clean segmentability | Morpheme boundaries are designed, not evolved |
| 17.35% fill rate (above natural language) | Not every combination has a real-world referent, but many do |
| Two scribes (17% vocabulary overlap) | Two practitioners using the same wheel system with slightly different personal conventions |
| Zipf's law compliance | Zipfian distribution of root frequencies (common plants discussed more) |
| No irregular morphology | Mechanical combination has no exceptions |
| Most frequent word is 5 chars ("daiin") | No separate function-word class; all words are category+item+modifier |
| Prefix-suffix correlation (ch- prefers -y, d- prefers -aiin) | Some category-modifier combinations are semantically valid and others are not (you don't apply every preparation to every substance) |

### 6.4 Why Not a Direct Cipher?

If the Voynich were simply an encoded natural language (as cipher hypotheses propose), we would expect:
- Irregular morphology (natural languages have it)
- Paradigm completeness of 30-60%
- Function words that are shorter than content words
- Dialect differences that are phonological, not morphological

The Voynich shows NONE of these natural-language properties. It shows the properties of a **designed combinatorial system**.

## 8. Objections and Counterarguments

### 8.1 "No Known Pseudo-Lullian Text Uses an Invented Script"

**True.** All known pseudo-Lullian texts use Latin with standard cipher elements, not constructed scripts. The Voynich would be unique in this respect.

**Counter**: The Voynich may not use a "script" at all in the conventional sense. If the Voynichese characters represent **morphemic components** rather than phonemes, the "script" is actually a **notation system** -- more like chemical formulae than an alphabet. The pseudo-Lullian tradition used letters-as-abbreviations (each letter = one alchemical principle), which is conceptually identical to Voynichese glyphs-as-morphemes.

### 8.2 "The Voynich Has Natural-Language-Like Statistics (Zipf, Entropy)"

**True.** But our simulation (Test 6 in the constructed notation analysis) proved that a combinatorial notation with Zipfian root frequencies PRODUCES Zipf's law and natural-language-like entropy. This objection is eliminated.

### 8.3 "The Voynich Has 72.9% Root Fusion, Too High for a Constructed System"

**Partially valid.** The root variation could indicate:
1. Roots derived from abbreviated real plant/substance names (inheriting their irregularity)
2. Sandhi rules at morpheme boundaries (designed complexity)
3. Over-segmentation by the analysis algorithm

Under the Llullian hypothesis, explanation (1) is most likely: the "middle wheel" elements are abbreviations of actual pharmaceutical names, not arbitrary symbols. They would naturally show variation.

### 8.4 "Llull's System Is for Theology, Not Pharmacy"

**False.** Llull himself wrote medical works (Ars compendiosa medicinae, Liber de regionibus sanitatis). The pseudo-Lullian Testamentum includes ~50 medical recipes. The application of Llullian method to pharmaceutical knowledge is documented.

### 8.5 "The Circular Diagrams Don't Look Like Llullian Wheels"

**Partially true.** The Voynich's circular diagrams are more illustrative than functional. But:
1. The rosettes foldout (9 circles connected by causeways) resembles a **map of categorical relationships** rather than individual wheels
2. If the text IS the output of a wheel system, the diagrams may show the **framework** rather than the **tool**
3. Pseudo-Lullian manuscripts use circles for symbolic/cosmological purposes (perfection, cyclical transformation) as well as for combinatorial generation
